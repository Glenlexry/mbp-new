Mr Sreether Sundaram

LL.B. (Hons.) London; LLM Hull; Barrister-at-Law, Middle Temple, London

Mr Sreether Sundaram graduated from the University of London (external programme) with an Honours Degree in Law in 1990, went on to obtain a LL.M in International Business Law from the University of Hull in 1991 before being called to the English Bar in 1992. He then stayed on in London as an Academic Administrator at Holborn College till September 1993. He read in chambers with M/S Soo Thien Ming & Shahrizat, Kuala Lumpur from 1993 till he was called to the Malaysian bar in 1994.

BACKGROUND

Upon his admission to the Bar, he joined M/S Adam & Co. as a Legal Assistant dealing solely with litigation matters, many of which were of a defensive nature. In 1995 he returned to the Petaling Jaya Branch of M/S Soo Thien Ming & Nashrah to head the Litigation Department there, dealing mainly with hire purchase, foreclosure, winding up and bankruptcy matters. In 1999 he was moved up to the Head Office of M/S Soo Thien Ming & Nashrah in Kuala Lumpur where he specialized in all facets of banking law till March 2001, when he moved to head the Kuala Lumpur Branch of M/S Balendran Chong & Bodi. He headed the Kuala Lumpur Branch of M/S Balendran Chong & Bodi from 2001 to 2002 before joining this firm as a partner in September 2002.

PRACTICE AREA

In his 20 years of practice he has conducted heavily contested civil suits involving all facets of banking law, corporate litigation and building contract dispatches. He has also gained experience in probate, insurance, matrimonial and industrial court matters. Extensive research, drafting of legal opinions and the conduct of matters in Court on a variety of matters such as hire purchase (including cases involving stolen vehicles/vehicles with tampered chassis/engine numbers/vehicles confiscated by customs/police), leasing, Insolvency, factoring agreements, Insurance matters, Company law, recovery of debts for goods sold and services rendered and general banking matters, have been part and parcel of his career as a litigator.

His rapport with clients in the banking sector have also afforded him the opportunity to participate actively in settlement negotiations with debtors and also to conduct Legal Seminars for bank officers on various aspects of banking law and debt recovery, including the different modes of execution available and the practical aspects of the law on Guarantees and Foreclosure. Besides attendances in the Subordinate Courts and High Courts in Kuala Lumpur, Kuantan, Ipoh, Temerloh, Taiping, Johor, Raub, Seremban and Kajang he has also enjoyed attendance the Court of Appeal and the Federal Court of Malaya.

Taking the lead in the reported case of Bank Bumiputra v Blue Valley Plantations & Ors, gave him the opportunity to specialize in various aspects of the technical and substantive law in foreclosure, including the effect of antecedent caveats on the indefeasibility of a charge, the effect of alleged misrepresentation by the bank and its former solicitors in an application to declare a charge as being void for forgery, the rights of purchasers for value against that of the chargee, the rights of the registered proprietor as against the chargee in Land Acquisition proceedings, the issues raised in sub division and partition of land subject to the governance of the Estate Land Board.
