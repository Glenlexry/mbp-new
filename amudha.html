Partners

Ms. Amudha Jayaraman

LL.M., LL.B. (Hons.) (Malaya)

Ms. Amudha graduated from the University of Malaya, Kuala Lumpur in 1993 with Honours. She then read in the chambers of Ms. Juliana Solomon of Messrs. Murali B. Pillai And Associates. She was called to the Bar in May 1994 and commenced practice in the Firm as a legal assistant.

BACKGROUND

During her chambering period with the Firm and since her call to the Bar, Ms. Amudha has been extensively involved in all manner and aspects of civil and commercial litigation, including: -

  • Corporate Litigation involving shareholders’ dispute and minority oppression suits;
  • Insurance matters including performance bonds, guarantees, agency debt recoveries, recovery, subrogation claims against third parties, defending insurance companies against claim under policies and acting for the insured in actions by third parties;
  • General Debt collection;
  • Banking;
  • Execution proceedings including bankruptcy and winding-up;
  • Tortious claim including Defamation;
  • Injunction.

PRACTICE AREA

Ms. Amudha has appeared in all levels of court in attending to the above matters including the Court of Appeal and Federal Court and has also appeared for the insurance companies before the Financial Mediation Bureau. Ms. Amudha specialises in insurance law and has advised and acted for insurance companies on: -

a. Agency

  • Recovery of premiums, including setting up standard procedures and documentation for the same;
  • Setting up standard agency agreements and documentation for appointment of agents;
  • Drafting of specialised agency agreement; and
  • Generally advising client as all areas, including regulatory aspect of agency.

b. Policies

  • Advising insurance companies on their liability to claims under all classes of policies, including fire, burglary, personal accident, all risks, contractors’ all risks, motor, life, fidelity guarantee, machinery and equipment workmen compensation, public liability, consequential loss medical and specially drafted policies;
  • Advising clients on prospects of recovery in subrogation claims;
  • Advising clients on insured’s liability to third party when policy liability to Insured is clear.

c. Fraudulent Claims

  • Advising insurance companies in how to conduct claims suspected of being fraudulent and advising clients on investigations which need to be conducted;
  • Working together with adjusters, forensic scientist and accountant in detecting fraudulent claims.

d. Drafting and Vetting of: -

  • Policies;
  • Discharge vouchers – receipt discharge indemnity and subrogation;
  • Performance bonds and advance payment bonds;Letters of guarantee;
  • Letters of indemnity on payments made by insurance companies.

e. Regulatory aspects of the insurance industry

  • Ms Amudha has been invited to give briefings to several insurance companies on insurance regulations, including the Insurance Act 1996.

Ms Amudha was made a partner of the Firm on 6th September 1999.
